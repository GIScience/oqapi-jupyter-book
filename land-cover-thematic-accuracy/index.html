Land Cover Thematic Accuracy

This indicator evaluates the agreement between OSM land cover data and the CORINE Land Cover (CLC) dataset. It can be applied either across multiple CLC classes or for selected classes individually.

OSM features are assigned to CLC Level 2 classes based on their landuse=* and natural=* tags. The spatial intersection of OSM polygons with CLC polygons produces composite geometries carrying both the OSM- and CLC-derived class labels. This step ensures that discrepancies in class definitions can be directly analyzed at the polygon level. Areas where one of the datasets is not available are ignored. For the defined area of interest, polygons resulting from the intersection of OSM and CLC data are aggregated into a confusion matrix. From this, we calculate class- specific and overall accuracy metrics, including precision, recall, and the F1 score. All metrics are area-weighted, ensuring that the spatial extent of polygons is taken into account rather than treating features as equally weighted units. This approach enables a systematic evaluation of the thematic consistency of OSM land cover data with respect to authoritative CLC reference data. The result value is calculated as the area-weighted F1- score normalised to a range of 0 to 1. Values below 0.6 are considered low thematic agreement, values between 0.6 and 0.85 as medium agreement and values above 0.85 as high agreement between OSM and CLC.

CORINE Land Cover¶

In its current form, the CORINE Land Cover (CLC) product offers a pan-European land cover and land use inventory with 44 thematic classes, ranging from broad forested areas to individual vineyards. CORINE uses a 3-level nomenclature for land cover classes. Here, we use the “CORINE Land Cover 5 ha, Stand 2021 (CLC5-2021)” provided by the German Federal Agency for Cartography and Geodesy and level 2 of the nomenclature (e.g. 1.1 Urban Fabric, 1.2 Industrial, commercial and transport units).

Preprocessing:¶

OSM features are assigned a CLC class based on their tags as specified below. We calculate the intersection of OSM land cover polygons and CORINE land cover polygons. The resulting polygons contain both the OSM CLC class and the CORINE class.
